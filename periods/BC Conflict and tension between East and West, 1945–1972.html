The Potsdam Conference was held to discuss the post-war reorganization of Europe and address issues of German reconstruction, but tensions between the Western Allies and the Soviet Union foreshadowed the onset of the Cold War.

Winston Churchill delivered his "Iron Curtain" speech in Fulton, Missouri, warning of the division of Europe into Eastern and Western blocs and advocating for solidarity among Western democracies against Soviet expansionism.

The Marshall Plan, officially known as the European Recovery Program, was announced to provide economic aid to Western European countries devastated by World War II, aiming to prevent the spread of communism and stabilize the region.

The Berlin Airlift commenced in response to the Soviet blockade of West Berlin, delivering vital supplies to the besieged city and demonstrating Western resolve to resist Soviet aggression.

The North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) was established as a collective defense alliance among Western democracies, providing a counterbalance to Soviet influence and ensuring security against communist aggression in Europe.

The Korean War erupted when North Korean forces invaded South Korea, leading to a bloody conflict between communist and Western-backed forces that ended in an armistice and the division of the Korean peninsula.

The Warsaw Pact was established as a military alliance among Soviet-aligned Eastern European countries in response to the creation of NATO, solidifying the division of Europe into opposing blocs during the Cold War.

The Cuban Missile Crisis brought the United States and the Soviet Union to the brink of nuclear war when the discovery of Soviet missile sites in Cuba led to a tense standoff and a negotiated resolution to remove the missiles.

The Vietnam War escalated when the Gulf of Tonkin incident prompted increased U.S. military involvement in Vietnam, leading to a protracted conflict between communist forces in North Vietnam and the U.S.-backed government in South Vietnam.

Detente refers to a period of reduced tension and improved relations between the United States and the Soviet Union during the late 1960s and early 1970s, marked by arms control agreements and diplomatic dialogue despite ongoing Cold War rivalry.
