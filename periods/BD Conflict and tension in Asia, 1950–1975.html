The Korean War began when North Korean forces invaded South Korea, leading to a conflict between communist-backed North Korea and Western-backed South Korea, with intervention from United Nations forces.

The Geneva Accords were signed to resolve the First Indochina War, leading to the partition of Vietnam into North and South Vietnam, with a promise of reunification through democratic elections that never took place.

The Southeast Asia Treaty Organization (SEATO) was established as a defense alliance among Southeast Asian countries and Western powers, aiming to contain the spread of communism in the region following the Korean War and French Indochina War.

The Vietnam War escalated when U.S. combat troops were deployed to South Vietnam, increasing American involvement in the conflict against communist forces in North Vietnam and the Viet Cong insurgency in the South.

The Cultural Revolution was launched by Mao Zedong to purge perceived bourgeois elements and revive revolutionary fervor in China, leading to widespread political upheaval, persecution, and economic disruption.

The Tet Offensive was a major offensive launched by North Vietnamese and Viet Cong forces against South Vietnam and U.S. allies during the Lunar New Year, shocking the American public and undermining confidence in the Vietnam War effort.

The My Lai Massacre occurred when U.S. soldiers massacred hundreds of unarmed Vietnamese civilians in a hamlet in South Vietnam, sparking outrage and contributing to growing anti-war sentiment in the United States.

The Paris Peace Accords were signed to end direct U.S. involvement in the Vietnam War, leading to a ceasefire and the withdrawal of American troops from Vietnam, though fighting continued between North and South Vietnam.

The Fall of Saigon marked the end of the Vietnam War and the reunification of Vietnam under communist rule, as North Vietnamese forces captured the capital of South Vietnam, leading to the collapse of the South Vietnamese government.

The Khmer Rouge, led by Pol Pot, seized power in Cambodia, initiating a brutal regime that resulted in widespread atrocities, forced labor, and the deaths of millions of Cambodians during the period known as the Cambodian Genocide.
