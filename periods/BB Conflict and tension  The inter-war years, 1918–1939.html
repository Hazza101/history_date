The Treaty of Versailles was signed, officially ending World War I and imposing severe penalties on Germany, leading to resentment and economic hardship that contributed to the rise of Adolf Hitler and World War II.

The Russian Revolution saw the overthrow of the Tsarist regime and the establishment of a Bolshevik government led by Vladimir Lenin, leading to civil war and the emergence of the Soviet Union as a global power.

The Washington Naval Conference was convened to address naval disarmament and reduce tensions among major powers, resulting in agreements to limit naval armaments and preserve peace in the Pacific.

The Dawes Plan was implemented to address Germany's inability to meet reparations payments, providing loans and restructuring payments to stabilize the German economy and promote recovery in Europe.

The Locarno Treaties were signed to secure the post-war borders of Europe and reduce tensions between Germany and its neighbors, laying the foundation for a period of relative stability known as the Locarno era.

The Great Depression began with the Wall Street Crash of 1929, triggering a global economic downturn that exacerbated social unrest, political extremism, and international tensions in the inter-war years.

The Manchurian Crisis began when Japanese forces invaded Manchuria, leading to condemnation by the League of Nations and undermining international efforts to maintain peace and security.

Adolf Hitler was appointed Chancellor of Germany, marking the beginning of the Nazi regime and the erosion of democratic institutions as Hitler consolidated power and pursued aggressive expansionist policies.

Germany remilitarized the Rhineland in violation of the Treaty of Versailles and international agreements, challenging the post-war order and escalating tensions in Europe.

Germany annexed Austria in the Anschluss, uniting the two German-speaking nations and furthering Hitler's expansionist ambitions, leading to increased international alarm and paving the way for the outbreak of World War II.
