Iraq invaded Kuwait, leading to the Gulf War, after disputes over oil production quotas and war debts, triggering international condemnation and United Nations sanctions against Iraq.

Operation Desert Storm, a coalition-led military operation authorized by the United Nations, commenced to liberate Kuwait from Iraqi occupation, marking the beginning of the Gulf War.

Osama bin Laden, leader of al-Qaeda, issued a fatwa declaring a holy war against the United States, citing U.S. military presence in Saudi Arabia and perceived injustices against Muslims as justification for attacks.

The Taliban, an Islamist militant group, captured the Afghan capital of Kabul, establishing a repressive regime that enforced strict Islamic law and harbored terrorist organizations, including al-Qaeda.

Coordinated bombings targeting U.S. embassies in Nairobi, Kenya, and Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, were carried out by al-Qaeda, killing hundreds of people and prompting U.S. retaliatory strikes against terrorist training camps in Afghanistan.

The September 11 attacks were a series of coordinated terrorist attacks by al-Qaeda on the United States, involving hijacked airplanes crashing into the World Trade Center in New York City, the Pentagon in Arlington, Virginia, and a failed attempt targeting Washington, D.C., killing thousands of people.

Following the September 11 attacks, U.S. and coalition forces launched Operation Enduring Freedom to overthrow the Taliban regime in Afghanistan and dismantle al-Qaeda terrorist networks, leading to a protracted military campaign and nation-building efforts.

The United States and its allies launched the invasion of Iraq, known as Operation Iraqi Freedom, citing the presence of weapons of mass destruction and alleged ties to terrorism as justification, leading to the overthrow of Saddam Hussein's regime.

The Abu Ghraib scandal involved the abuse and torture of prisoners by U.S. military personnel at the Abu Ghraib prison in Iraq, resulting in international condemnation and calls for accountability and reform of U.S. detention policies.

President George W. Bush announced a surge of U.S. troops in Iraq to quell sectarian violence and stabilize the country, resulting in a temporary reduction in violence but failing to achieve long-term stability or reconciliation among Iraqi factions.
