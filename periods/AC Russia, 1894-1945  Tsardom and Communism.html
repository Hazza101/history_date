AC Russia, 1894-1945: Tsardom and Communism

Exam Board: AQA History

Paper: Paper 1 (Understanding the Modern World)

Section: Section A (Period Studies)

The coronation of Nicholas II marked the culmination of Russia's autocratic rule under the Romanov dynasty, setting the stage for his troubled reign and eventual downfall.

Bloody Sunday was a peaceful protest march in St. Petersburg that ended in a violent crackdown by the Imperial Guard, fueling revolutionary sentiments and triggering widespread unrest across Russia.

The Russian Revolution began with strikes and demonstrations in Petrograd, leading to the overthrow of the Tsarist regime and the establishment of a provisional government followed by the Bolshevik seizure of power in October.

The execution of Tsar Nicholas II and his family by the Bolsheviks marked the end of the Romanov dynasty and symbolized the brutality of the Russian Civil War and the establishment of communist rule.

The formation of the Soviet Union brought together multiple republics and ethnic groups under communist rule, establishing a centralized government led by the Bolshevik Party and laying the groundwork for socialist transformation.

Stalin's Five-Year Plans aimed to rapidly industrialize the Soviet economy and collectivize agriculture, resulting in significant social and economic upheaval, including mass forced labor and famine.

The Great Purge, orchestrated by Joseph Stalin, targeted political opponents, intellectuals, and perceived enemies of the state, resulting in widespread repression, imprisonment, and execution of millions of people.

The Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact was a non-aggression treaty between the Soviet Union and Nazi Germany, allowing for the partition of Eastern Europe and setting the stage for the outbreak of World War II.

The German invasion of the Soviet Union, known as Operation Barbarossa, caught Stalin's regime by surprise and led to devastating losses for the Red Army, but ultimately resulted in the Soviet Union's victory and the turning point of World War II.

The end of World War II brought victory for the Soviet Union and its allies, solidifying the Soviet Union's status as a global superpower and shaping the post-war division of Europe.
