BA Norman England, c1066–c1100

Exam Board: AQA History

Paper: Paper 2 (Shaping the Nation)

Section: Section B (British Depth Studies including the Historic Environment)

The Battle of Hastings took place between the Norman-French army of William the Conqueror and the English forces led by King Harold II, resulting in the decisive Norman victory and the subsequent Norman conquest of England.

The Domesday Book, a comprehensive survey of landholdings and resources in England, was completed under the direction of William the Conqueror, providing valuable insights into the social, economic, and administrative structures of Norman England.

The Normans constructed numerous castles across England, including the Tower of London, Rochester Castle, and Durham Castle, as strategic strongholds to consolidate their control over the newly conquered territory and assert Norman authority.

The Normans introduced the feudal system to England, where land was granted by the king to nobles (tenants-in-chief) in exchange for military service and loyalty, leading to a hierarchical society with distinct social classes and obligations.

William the Conqueror conducted the Harrying of the North, a brutal campaign of scorched-earth tactics and suppression against rebellious northern English regions, resulting in widespread devastation, famine, and loss of life.

The Normans implemented church reforms in England, including the appointment of Norman bishops and the imposition of ecclesiastical control, leading to the integration of English and Norman religious institutions and practices.

The Normans introduced legal reforms in England, including the development of a centralized royal court system and the compilation of legal codes such as the Laws of William the Conqueror, laying the foundations for English common law.

William the Conqueror was crowned King of England following his victory at the Battle of Hastings, marking the beginning of Norman rule and the Norman dynasty in England, and initiating significant political, social, and cultural changes.

The Normans initiated the construction of grand cathedrals in England, including Durham Cathedral, Winchester Cathedral, and Canterbury Cathedral, showcasing Norman architectural innovation and religious patronage.

The Bayeux Tapestry, an embroidered cloth depicting the Norman conquest of England and the events leading up to the Battle of Hastings, was created, providing a valuable historical record and artistic representation of Norman England.
