AC Britain: Migration, empires and the people: c790 to the present day

Exam Board: AQA History

Paper: Paper 2 (Shaping the Nation)

Section: Section A (Thematic Studies)

Viking raids on Britain commenced, marking the beginning of Norse invasions and settlements in British territories, leading to cultural exchange, conflict, and the eventual integration of Norse elements into British society.

The Battle of Hastings took place, resulting in the Norman conquest of England under William the Conqueror, leading to the establishment of Norman rule, feudalism, and the merging of Anglo-Saxon and Norman cultures.

British colonization efforts in the Americas expanded during the 16th to 18th centuries, leading to the establishment of colonies such as Jamestown in Virginia and the Thirteen Colonies, shaping transatlantic trade, migration patterns, and the growth of the British Empire.

The British Parliament passed the Abolition of the Slave Trade Act, outlawing the transatlantic slave trade in British territories, reflecting growing abolitionist sentiments and humanitarian concerns about the exploitation of enslaved Africans.

The partition of British India into the independent nations of India and Pakistan occurred, leading to widespread violence, displacement, and communal tensions between Hindus, Muslims, and Sikhs, and shaping the demographic landscape of South Asia.

The arrival of the Windrush generation, migrants from the Caribbean who came to Britain to fill labor shortages after World War II, contributed to Britain's cultural diversity and highlighted issues of immigration, citizenship, and racial discrimination.

The Empire Windrush, a ship carrying passengers from the Caribbean, docked in Britain, marking the beginning of post-war migration from the Commonwealth countries and symbolizing the start of large-scale immigration to Britain.

The Kenyan government expelled thousands of Asian residents, many of whom held British passports, leading to their resettlement in Britain, highlighting the complexities of colonial legacies, citizenship, and migration policies.

The Commonwealth Immigration Act was passed in Britain, restricting immigration from Commonwealth countries and introducing entry controls based on employment prospects and qualifications, reflecting concerns about population growth and economic competition.

The United Kingdom held a referendum on its membership in the European Union (EU), resulting in a narrow victory for the "Leave" campaign, leading to ongoing debates, negotiations, and political divisions surrounding Britain's withdrawal from the EU.
